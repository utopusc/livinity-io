**Year 1 Target:** $21.6K/year ($1.8K/mo)

2. **Enterprise support:** $100/incident or $500+/contract (1-2 = $1K/mo)

**Year 2 target:** $50K+/year (add app store commission, higher support)

## Solo Developer Burnout Prevention

**Sustainable:** 15-20h/week part-time OR 30h/week full-time (NOT 40h)

**Rules:**
1. Weekends off (no exceptions)
2. Ship on schedule, not perfection (every 2 weeks)
3. Say no to 80% of features
4. Automate repetitive tasks
5. Delegate early (mods by month 1, contributors by month 3)
6. Track hours (avoid silent drift to 40h/week)
7. Take breaks (1-2 weeks/year off, completely)

**Hiring timeline:**
- Month 0-3: Volunteer mods + doc contributors
- Month 3-6: First bounties ($500-1K)
- Month 6-12: First part-time hire ($2-5K/mo)

## Key Success Factors

1. **Launch momentum is everything**
   - Timing, channels, messaging, community seeding
   - Target: 500+ HN upvotes = trending = 1-3K stars in 24h

2. **Documentation is your moat**
   - Quality docs = adoption rate (30-50% improvement possible)
   - Video tutorials, FAQ, troubleshooting, API docs

3. **Community over features**
   - Discord > GitHub issues for culture
   - Volunteer mods, feature voting, transparent decisions

4. **Shipping builds momentum**
   - Every 2 weeks minimum (even small releases)
   - Transparency (share roadmap, challenges, metrics)

5. **Authenticity drives growth**
   - No hype, no sales pitch, genuine problem-solving
   - Share limitations, be honest about roadmap

## What NOT to Do

❌ Don't press-first (infrastructure products don't win via PR)
❌ Don't VC-first (creates unsustainable exit pressure)
❌ Don't monetize-first (premature, kills adoption)
❌ Don't burn out (open-source projects die from founder burnout)
❌ Don't close-source parts (breaks trust)
❌ Don't track telemetry (privacy violation, kills adoption)
❌ Don't compete with friends (partner instead: OpenWebUI, Home Assistant)
